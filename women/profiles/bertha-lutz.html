Bertha Lutz

Published on December 13, 2025
Bertha Lutz — Brazilian scientist and biologist (public domain)

Bertha Lutz (1894–1976) was a Brazilian biologist, scientist, and one of the most influential figures in the history of science and women's rights in Brazil.

Trained in biology at the Sorbonne in Paris, she worked as a researcher at the National Museum in Rio de Janeiro, specializing in amphibians and biodiversity, while also becoming a leading voice for gender equality in science and society.

Key Contributions

  • Specialist in amphibian taxonomy and Brazilian biodiversity
  • Researcher at the National Museum of Brazil
  • Founder of the Brazilian Federation for the Advancement of Women
  • Key advocate for women’s political and educational rights in Brazil
  • Member of the Brazilian delegation to the United Nations

Timeline

1894 — Born in São Paulo, Brazil
1918 — Graduated in biology from the Sorbonne (Paris)
1919 — Began working at the National Museum in Rio de Janeiro
1932 — Played a major role in securing women's right to vote in Brazil
1945 — Participated in the founding of the United Nations
1976 — Died in Rio de Janeiro

Why She Matters

Bertha Lutz showed that scientific excellence and social responsibility can walk side by side. Her legacy extends beyond biology, inspiring generations of women to claim space in laboratories, universities, and leadership positions across STEM fields.
