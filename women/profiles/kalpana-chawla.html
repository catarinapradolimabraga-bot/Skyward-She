Kalpana Chawla

Published on December 13, 2025
Kalpana Chawla in her NASA spacesuit — Public domain

Kalpana Chawla (1961–2003) was the first woman of Indian origin in space. A renowned aerospace engineer and astronaut, her career and legacy have inspired millions around the world.

She was part of the NASA Space Shuttle program and made history as a crew member of the Space Shuttle Columbia, which tragically disintegrated during re-entry in 2003.

Key Contributions

  • First woman of Indian origin in space
  • First woman to pilot a space shuttle
  • Involved in the Space Shuttle Columbia mission
  • Contributed to the development of advanced airfoil designs

Timeline

1961 — Born in Karnal, Haryana, India
1982 — Graduated with a degree in aeronautical engineering
1997 — First flight in space aboard Space Shuttle Columbia
2003 — Tragically died during the Space Shuttle Columbia disaster

Why She Matters

Kalpana Chawla's pioneering career in space exploration continues to inspire women and people of all backgrounds to pursue STEM fields, proving that determination and courage can break barriers, even in the most challenging environments.
