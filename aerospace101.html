Aerospace 101

A short, friendly introduction to basic aerospace concepts — perfect if you're starting out. Below you'll find simple explanations and quick links to deeper articles.

Quick basics

How rockets work

Rockets push themselves by throwing mass out the back very quickly (Newton's third law). Engines burn fuel to create high-speed exhaust that produces thrust.

What is an orbit?

An orbit is when an object moves sideways fast enough that it keeps “falling” around a planet instead of landing — gravity pulls it in, its forward speed keeps it circling.

Forces of flight

Lift, weight, thrust and drag are the four forces pilots and engineers balance when designing airplanes and spacecraft during atmospheric flight.

Satellites & telemetry

Satellites carry instruments and send data back to Earth (telemetry). They help us in communication, weather, navigation and science.

Reentry & heat

When spacecraft return from orbit they hit air at high speed and heat up. Special heat shields protect them by absorbing and shedding heat.

How to start small

Try simple projects: model rockets, build a balloon payload, track satellites with an app, or design experiments for school science fairs.
